Gluten-Free Living

Hi! My name is Sofia Pietropaolo. I am a 19-year-old student at Syracuse University. The summer before my freshman year, I was diagnosed with celiac disease. Although this came as quite a shock, it was a fairly easy transition as I already had a lot of experience with gluten free eating as well as cooking. This is my grandmother, Sandra Peavey. She has been gluten free for 15 years. When I was younger, she and my grandfather would watch my brother and me on Monday nights. Every week she would teach me a new gluten free recipe. My favorite part of the week was cooking with her. Now I get to share our love of gluten free cooking with others. Celiac vs Intolerance
Celiac Disease Gluten Intolerance
Stomach pain, bloating, constipation, diarrhea Stomach pain, bloating, constipation, diarrhea
Can lead to other autoimmune disorders like type 1 diabetes and multiple sclerosis, anemia, osteoporosis, infertility, neurological disorders, and cancer No autoimmune response and does not lead to long-term complications
Eating even tiny amounts of gluten can trigger symptoms and increase risk of developing complications Not affected by cross-contamination or tiny amounts of gluten in crumbs
Diagnosed with blood test and endoscopy Diagnosed by improved symptoms with a gluten-free diet and testing negative for celiac disease
